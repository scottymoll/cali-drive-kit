1. Pricing Guide & Worksheets

Understanding Your Car's Value

Pricing your car correctly is the most important factor in a successful sale. Price too high and you'll scare away buyers. Price too low and you'll leave money on the table.

Step 1: Research Market Value

  • Check Kelley Blue Book (KBB.com) for your car's value
  • Search similar cars on Craigslist, Facebook Marketplace, and AutoTrader
  • Look at cars within 50 miles of your location
  • Compare cars with similar mileage, year, and condition

Pricing Adjustments for California

California-Specific Factors:

  • Smog Check Status: Cars that need smog checks sell for less
  • Registration Status: Current registration adds value
  • Seasonal Demand: Convertibles sell better in spring/summer
  • Gas Prices: Fuel-efficient cars command premium in CA

2. Photo & Listing Blueprint

Photography Essentials

Great photos can make the difference between a car that sits for months and one that sells in days. Follow this blueprint for professional results.

Photo Shot List (Minimum 15 Photos)

  • Front 3/4 view (driver's side)
  • Rear 3/4 view (driver's side)
  • Side profile (entire car)
  • Front straight-on
  • Rear straight-on
  • Interior - driver's seat
  • Interior - dashboard
  • Interior - back seats
  • Engine bay
  • Trunk/cargo area
  • Wheels and tires
  • Any damage or wear
  • VIN plate (for verification)
  • Odometer reading
  • Title or registration

Photography Tips:

  • Clean your car thoroughly before shooting
  • Use natural light - early morning or late afternoon
  • Park on level ground with good background
  • Take photos from multiple angles
  • Don't use filters or heavy editing
  • Show any damage honestly

3. Buyer Screening Scripts

Effective screening saves you time and helps you identify serious buyers. Use these scripts to filter out tire-kickers and time-wasters.

Initial Contact Script (Text/Email)

Your Response:

"Hi [NAME], thanks for your interest in my [YEAR] [MAKE] [MODEL]. I have a few quick questions to make sure this is a good fit for both of us:

  • Are you looking to buy within the next week?
  • Do you have financing arranged or will you be paying cash?
  • What's your budget range?
  • When would you like to see the car?

If this sounds good, I'd be happy to schedule a viewing. Thanks!"

Phone Screening Questions

  • "What made you interested in this particular car?"
  • "Have you seen similar cars in your price range?"
  • "What's your timeline for purchasing?"
  • "Do you have any specific questions about the car?"
  • "Are you familiar with the smog check requirements in California?"

Red Flags to Watch For:

  • Asking to see the car immediately without questions
  • Offering to pay more than asking price
  • Asking about shipping or remote purchase
  • Vague answers about financing or timeline
  • Pressure to meet at unusual times or locations

4. Safe Test-Drive Protocol

Safety should be your top priority when meeting potential buyers. Follow this protocol to protect yourself and your property.

Pre-Meeting Checklist

  • Meet in a public, well-lit location during daylight hours
  • Bring a friend or family member with you
  • Take photos of your car before the meeting
  • Remove all personal items from the car
  • Have your phone charged and ready
  • Know the route you'll take for the test drive

Buyer Verification Process

  • Ask to see their driver's license
  • Verify their insurance covers test drives
  • Take a photo of their license (with permission)
  • Get their full name and phone number
  • Ask for a reference or proof of employment

Test Drive Agreement

I, [BUYER NAME], agree to the following terms for test driving [YEAR] [MAKE] [MODEL]:

  • I have a valid driver's license and insurance
  • I will drive safely and follow all traffic laws
  • I will not exceed 30 mph during the test drive
  • I will not smoke, eat, or drink in the vehicle
  • I understand I am responsible for any damage I cause
  • I will return the vehicle to the meeting location

Signature: _________________ Date: _____________

Safety Rules:

  • Never let them drive alone
  • Keep your keys with you at all times
  • Don't meet at your home address
  • Trust your instincts - if something feels wrong, end the meeting
  • Have an exit strategy planned

5. Offer & Negotiation Tactics

Negotiation is a skill that can save or cost you thousands of dollars. Use these tactics to get the best price while maintaining a positive relationship with the buyer.

Pre-Negotiation Preparation

  • Know your bottom line before you start
  • Research comparable sales in your area
  • Be prepared to justify your asking price
  • Have maintenance records ready to show
  • Consider what you're willing to include (extras, warranties, etc.)

Counter-Offer Scripts

For Lowball Offers:

"I appreciate your offer, but I've done my research and I'm confident in my asking price. The car is in excellent condition and priced competitively with similar vehicles in the area. I'm willing to negotiate, but we need to be closer to my asking price."

For Reasonable Offers:

"That's a fair offer. I was hoping to get closer to [YOUR COUNTER], but I understand your position. Would you be willing to meet me at [COMPROMISE PRICE]? I can throw in [EXTRA INCENTIVE] to make it worth your while."

For Multiple Offers:

"I have another interested buyer coming to see the car tomorrow. If you're serious about purchasing, I'd be happy to work with you today. What's the best offer you can make right now?"

Negotiation Do's and Don'ts

DO:

  • Stay calm and professional
  • Listen to their concerns
  • Be willing to compromise
  • Create urgency without pressure
  • Know when to walk away

DON'T:

  • Get emotional or defensive
  • Accept the first offer
  • Reveal your bottom line too early
  • Make threats or ultimatums
  • Rush the decision

6. Closeout & DMV Paperwork

Completing the sale properly protects you from future liability and ensures the buyer can register the car. Follow these steps exactly.

Pre-Sale Checklist

  • Gather all necessary documents
  • Complete smog check if required
  • Verify payment method and amount
  • Prepare Bill of Sale
  • Plan meeting location and time

Required Documents

Documents You Need:

  • Vehicle Title: Signed and dated by seller
  • Bill of Sale: Completed and signed by both parties
  • Smog Certificate: If required (see below)
  • Odometer Disclosure: If car is less than 10 years old
  • Notice of Transfer (REG 138): To protect seller from liability

Smog Check Requirements

California Smog Check Rules:

  • Required for: Most vehicles 8+ years old
  • Not required for: Vehicles 7 years old or newer
  • Who pays: Seller is responsible for smog check
  • Validity: Smog certificate is valid for 90 days
  • Cost: Typically $30-60 at certified stations

DMV Transfer Process

Steps for Buyer (You Don't Need to Do This):

  • Complete Notice of Transfer (REG 138) within 5 days
  • Visit DMV with all required documents
  • Pay registration fees and taxes
  • Receive new title and registration

Important: Notice of Transfer (REG 138)

You must complete and submit this form within 5 days of the sale to protect yourself from liability. This form notifies the DMV that you no longer own the vehicle.

Download at: dmv.ca.gov/forms/reg/reg138.pdf

Success Tips & Common Mistakes

Success Tips:

  • Be honest about your car's condition
  • Respond to inquiries quickly
  • Be flexible with meeting times
  • Keep your car clean during the selling process
  • Be patient - good buyers take time to find

Common Mistakes to Avoid:

  • Pricing too high or too low
  • Using poor quality photos
  • Not screening buyers properly
  • Rushing the paperwork
  • Not protecting yourself during test drives
  • Forgetting to submit Notice of Transfer
